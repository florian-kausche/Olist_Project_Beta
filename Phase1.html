01

Introduction

Data analytics is the systematic process of collecting, organizing, analyzing, and interpreting data to solve problems and generate insights that support decision-making.

This project uses the Brazilian Olist e-commerce dataset to analyze customer, order, payment, and geographical information. The objective is to understand the available data, identify governance requirements, and define the business problem that analytics will address. Proper data governance ensures that the data is accurate, secure, ethical, and suitable for analysis.

03

Business Problem Statement

The Olist e-commerce platform generates high volumes of customer, order, payment, and location data. Without proper analysis, it is difficult to identify customer purchasing patterns, understand payment preferences, evaluate shipping costs, and improve operational performance.

Objective — use data analytics to generate insights that support better business decisions, improve customer satisfaction, optimize logistics, and enhance overall business performance.

04

Data Inventory

Four datasets carry the core of the analysis: who the customer is, where they are, what they bought, and how they paid for it.

Customer Dataset

01

Identifies each customer and their general location for regional and delivery analysis.

  • customer_id
  • customer_unique_id
  • zip_code_prefix
  • city
  • state
Types — Text (IDs) · Numeric (ZIP prefix) · Categorical (city, state)

Geolocation Dataset

02

Maps ZIP code prefixes to geographic coordinates, enabling spatial and regional analysis.

  • zip_code_prefix
  • latitude
  • longitude
  • city
  • state
Types — Numeric (coordinates) · Categorical (city, state)

Order Items Dataset

03

Transactional detail for every product purchased, linking orders to products and sellers.

  • order_id
  • product_id
  • seller_id
  • shipping_deadline
  • price
Types — Text (IDs) · Date (shipping deadline) · Numeric (price)

Order Payments Dataset

04

Records how each order was paid for, including method and installment structure.

  • order_id
  • payment_type
  • installments
  • payment_value
Types — Text (ID) · Categorical (payment type) · Numeric (installments, value)
05

Privacy & Ethical Issues

Although the datasets do not contain customer names or contact details, they include location information and unique customer identifiers. These elements should be handled responsibly to protect privacy.

!

Key ethical considerations

  • Using customer data only for legitimate business purposes
  • Restricting access to sensitive datasets
  • Reporting only aggregated results instead of individual customer information
  • Preventing the identification of individual customers
06

Data Governance

Effective data governance ensures that data remains secure, accurate, and accessible throughout the analytics lifecycle.

Role-based access control

Limit dataset access to individuals whose responsibilities require it.

Encryption in storage and transit

Protect sensitive data at rest and while it moves between systems.

Audit logging

Keep a record of data access and modifications for accountability.

Defined data ownership

Assign clear ownership and responsibility for each dataset.
